Born to Fly, Not to Decorate

Let Birds Be Birds

Birds are intelligent, sensitive, and highly social animals, yet they are often treated as decorations or objects. Many people keep birds without fully understanding their true needs.

Beyond the Cage was created to help change that.

This website is dedicated to educating bird owners about responsible bird care, including proper nutrition, enrichment, housing, and respect for birds as living companions.

Why Beyond the Cage Exists

Many birds today live in environments that do not meet their physical or psychological needs. Birds in captivity often suffer from boredom, stress, and poor diets simply because people do not know how to care for them properly.

Beyond the Cage was created to provide clear and accessible information so that bird owners can understand what their birds truly need in order to live healthy and fulfilling lives.

The goal of this website is to encourage responsible bird ownership and remind people that birds are not decorations or toys. They are intelligent living creatures that deserve proper care, respect, and the freedom to express their natural behaviors.

My Story

One day I visited a bird shop because I wanted to learn more about birds and possibly bring one home. While I was there, I noticed something that disturbed me. I saw a bird lying in a bin and asked the worker why it was there. The worker casually told me that the bird had been thrown away simply because he did not like its color.

As I continued looking around the shop, I realized that many birds were being treated as products rather than living animals. Some cages were extremely overcrowded, with dozens of birds placed in very small spaces. In some cages, birds were even lying at the bottom because there was not enough room or proper care.

Eventually, I decided to bring home a cockatiel. When I bought the bird, the shop owner told me to feed it only seeds. This is advice that many bird shops still give to customers. However, seed-only diets are not healthy for birds because they are extremely high in fat and lack important nutrients.

Another problem I noticed was how the seeds were stored. The shop kept large containers of seeds exposed to dust, insects, and contamination. Poor storage and hygiene can make food unsafe for birds, and many people unknowingly feed their birds food that is not fresh or properly handled.

When I asked questions about the birds, the answers focused mostly on their colors and prices rather than their species, needs, or personalities. It became clear that many people selling birds did not fully understand how to care for them properly.

That experience made me realize how much misinformation exists about bird care. Birds are intelligent and sensitive animals that require proper diets, mental stimulation, and space to thrive.

I created Beyond the Cage to help people learn the right way to care for birds and to encourage responsible bird ownership.

Birds are not decorations. They are living companions that deserve knowledge, care, and respect.
